Master’s Thesis

Entanglement Studies in the AKLT Spin-1 Biquadratic Model

This thesis presents a numerical investigation of quantum entanglement in the Affleck–Kennedy–Lieb–Tasaki (AKLT) spin-1 biquadratic model. Entanglement measures such as Wootters’ concurrence and von Neumann entropy were used to analyze bipartite correlations. The ground-state properties were verified to be singlet in nature, and signatures of quantum phase transitions were explored using optimized numerical techniques and parallel computation.

Institution: Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology, Nagpur

Supervisor: Dr. M. S. Ramkarthik

Research Projects

Quantum Entanglement in Multi-Qubit Systems

Summer Research Fellowship project at IIT Dharwad focused on detecting quantum entanglement in multi-qubit systems using the covariance matrix criterion. The work involved extending the criterion to higher-dimensional systems and understanding its limitations in quantifying entanglement.

Radio Astronomy and RGBC Image Analysis

Research work under the RAD@Home program involving the analysis of extragalactic radio sources through RGBC image construction. Multi-wavelength FITS data were analyzed to study morphological features and identify unusual radio galaxy structures.

Positional Astronomy at Jantar Mantar

Observational project centered on historical astronomical instruments at Jantar Mantar, New Delhi. The work included calculating the Sun’s altitude and azimuth, determining local time for multiple locations, and understanding the working principles of traditional Indian astronomical instruments.
