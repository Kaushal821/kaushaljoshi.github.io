Research Trajectory

  • Early training in quantum mechanics, mathematical physics, and classical field theory.
  • MSc thesis focused on entanglement properties of AKLT spin-1 systems.
  • Exposure to quantum information theory, covariance matrix criteria, and multi-qubit entanglement.
  • Current PhD research centered on quantum entanglement, many-body spin systems, and numerical simulations.

Technical & Computational Skills

  • Programming: Fortran, Python, Bash, C, C++

  • Parallel computing: MPI, OpenACC (GPU acceleration)
  • Scientific tools: LaTeX, Git, Mathematica, Linux-based workflows

Current Research Focus

My current research focuses on understanding quantum correlations and entanglement in interacting spin systems. I am particularly interested in the role of information-theoretic quantities in characterizing quantum phases and thermodynamic behavior in many-body systems.

Future Directions

I aim to further explore the intersection of quantum information theory and quantum thermodynamics, with an emphasis on scalable numerical methods and analytical insights applicable to strongly correlated quantum systems.
